One Nation One Election • 2029

What is One Nation, One Election?

Currently, India witnesses elections in different states every few months. ONOE (One Nation, One Election) proposes to synchronize the Lok Sabha (Parliament) and all State Assembly elections to occur simultaneously once every 5 years, starting June 2029.

How to read this map: Green states will have their current term extended to meet the 2029 deadline. Red states will have their term cut short (curtailed) to align with the 2029 vote.

Instrument
The Constitution (129th Amendment) Bill
Requires two-thirds majority in both Houses. Key amendments listed below.
Required Amendments
Modifications needed for legal validity.
Article 83 & 172 Pending
Duration of Houses: Fixes term of Lok Sabha and State Assemblies to 5 years from "Appointed Date".
Article 85 & 174 Pending
Dissolution: Defines powers of the President and Governors to dissolve Houses in sync with the cycle.
Article 356 Pending
Remainder Term: If a state govt falls mid-term, the new govt serves only the remaining period.
Article 325 Drafting
Single Voter List: Mandates common electoral roll for Parliament, Assembly, and Local bodies.
Article 324A Pending
ECI Powers: Empowers Election Commission to conduct simultaneous polls despite logistical hurdles.
Tenth Schedule In Review
Anti-Defection: Stricter rules to prevent government collapse, ensuring the 5-year fixed term.
Ratification Process
State Approval: Amendments affecting federal structure require ratification by 50% of states.
Strategic Analysis
Core arguments regarding ONOE implementation.
Financial Impact
Proponent View

Saves approx ₹45,000 Cr per cycle by combining logistics. Security forces (CAPF) are deployed once, reducing transport and allowance costs significantly.

Opposing View

Buying 3x EVMs/VVPATs for simultaneous use creates a massive 'sunk cost' (approx ₹10,000 Cr). Electronic units have a limited shelf life of 15 years.

Governance & Policy
Proponent View

Eliminates the Model Code of Conduct (MCC) paralysis. Governments get 5 uninterrupted years for infrastructure execution without populist pauses.

Opposing View

Frequent elections force accountability. Without mid-term polls, governments may become unresponsive or "sleep" on issues for 4 years.

Federal Structure
Proponent View

Strengthens national unity. Reduces the "perpetual campaign mode" of the PM and CMs, allowing them to focus on administration.

Opposing View

National waves tend to overshadow local issues. Voters may vote for the same party for PM and CM, hurting regional parties.

Operational Feasibility
Proponent View

Streamlines the electoral roll. Teachers and administrative staff are not disrupted repeatedly for election duty every few months.

Opposing View

Managing security for 1.4 billion people on a single timeline is a logistical nightmare. A single glitch could delay the entire nation.
